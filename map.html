Hotspot Map

This map shows the per-capita prevalence of COVID-19 across the UK for the last seven days that reliable data is available. The data from England and Scotland are derived from different sources, and are not directly comparable. For this reason they are shown in different colours.

In England, the map shows the last week's number of positive "pillar 1" COVID-19 cases per capita, by local authority district — these are positive tests in hospital patients or NHS staff, and so represent a small proportion of total cases. Nonetheless, it should be representative of the relative prevalence of COVID-19 across England.

English data is reported by date of sample, so the most recent four days of data are ignored as they may be incomplete. This map covers the seven days ending on 2020-06-15.

In Scotland, the government statistics are aggregated by local health board, and also include "pillar 2" tests of people showing COVID-19 symptoms outside of hospitals, so this data should be more complete. Pillar 2 data was added to the Scottish numbers on 15 July, and a crude correction has been applied to smooth this discontinuity.

You can click on an area on the map to see the current numbers and a history of the last 45 days of daily cases.
